Favourite Cities

Lahore

Lahore, often referred to as the "Heart of Pakistan," is a vibrant and culturally rich city that stands as the capital of the Punjab province. With a history dating back over a millennium, Lahore is a treasure trove of architectural wonders, including the iconic Badshahi Mosque, Lahore Fort, and Shalimar Gardens, which reflect the city's Mughal heritage.

Faisalabad

Faisalabad, often referred to as the "Manchester of Pakistan," is a bustling industrial city located in the heart of the country's Punjab province. Originally known as Lyallpur, the city was named after Sir James Lyall, the then Lieutenant Governor of the province during British rule. Faisalabad is a major center for the textile industry, housing numerous spinning mills and weaving facilities.

Islamabad

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, stands as a symbol of modernity, planning, and elegance. Nestled at the foothills of the Margalla Hills, Islamabad was chosen as the capital in 1963, replacing Karachi. The city is known for its well-planned infrastructure, greenery, and serene surroundings.

Tourist Spots

Badshahi Mosque

A masterpiece of Mughal architecture, the Badshahi Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world. Completed in 1673, it is known for its stunning red sandstone and marble construction. The mosque is a symbol of grandeur and magnificence.

Lahore Fort

Adjacent to the Badshahi Mosque, the Lahore Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It showcases a blend of Mughal, Sikh, and British architectural influences. Visitors can explore the Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), Alamgiri Gate, and the beautifully landscaped Shalimar Gardens within the fort complex.

Shalimar Garden

Built during the reign of Emperor Shah Jahan, the Shalimar Gardens are a prime example of Mughal garden design. The terraced gardens feature flowing water, fountains, and pavilions, creating a serene and picturesque atmosphere.

Sheesh Mahal

Located within the Lahore Fort, the Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors) is a stunning structure adorned with intricate mirror work. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan and is a captivating display of craftsmanship and artistic excellence.

Anarkali Bazaar

One of the oldest markets in Lahore, Anarkali Bazaar is a bustling marketplace known for its vibrant atmosphere and diverse array of shops. Visitors can explore a variety of goods, including traditional clothing, jewelry, and handicrafts.

Lahore Museum

Founded in 1865, the Lahore Museum is one of the oldest museums in South Asia. It houses an extensive collection of artifacts, including manuscripts, coins, sculptures, and exhibits that showcase the region's rich history.
